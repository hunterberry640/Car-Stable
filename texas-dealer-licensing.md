# Car Stable — Licensing Roadmap

> ⚠️ **This doc is operational planning, not legal advice.** Before taking a single customer dollar, have a Texas dealer-compliance attorney review the Year 0 structure in writing. Budget $1,000-$2,500 for a one-time consult. Specifically ask: does providing concierge auction-buying services for a fee — where Car Stable never takes title — require a Texas dealer or broker license? The answer shapes everything.

---

## TL;DR — The Plan

| Phase | Timing | Legal structure | Volume ceiling | Revenue model |
|-------|--------|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Year 0** | Months 1-12 | Car Stable LLC is a **service company**. FL partner executes all auction purchases under their dealer license; title briefly held by FL partner, then transferred to customer. Car Stable charges a separate service fee and never takes title. | No hard statutory cap; stay lean (~2-3 deals/month) until attorney review confirms the structure | Flat service fees |
| **Year 1+** | Month 12+ | Car Stable's own **Texas GDN** (Independent Motor Vehicle Dealer). We buy at auction under our own dealer account, briefly hold title, reassign to customer. No inventory held. | Unlimited | Flat service fees |

**The business in both phases is the same:** concierge car buying, auction pricing, transparent flat fee, no markup. The only thing that changes is whose dealer license is on the paperwork.

## Year 0 — FL Partner / Service Company Structure

### Why this is cleaner than the old private-party model

In the previous model (private-party sales under named individuals), Car Stable's owners were the legal sellers of vehicles — which had volume caps, salvage limits, and "bought-to-flip" risk.

In this model:
- **Car Stable LLC never takes title** — we provide a service, not a vehicle
- **The FL partner is the licensed dealer** who executes the purchase and title transfer
- **The customer is the ultimate buyer** — they commission us to find their car
- **Car Stable's revenue is a service fee** — independent of the vehicle transaction

This is meaningfully different from dealing in vehicles, which is the regulated activity. Whether Texas still requires a license for this service-company model is the key attorney question.

### The three parties

**Car Stable LLC (Texas)**
- Finds the vehicle (searches live auction inventory; delivers shortlist to customer)
- Manages the customer relationship entirely — intake, shortlist, approval, communication, post-delivery support
- Coordinates add-on services: rebuild management, financing referrals, transport
- Charges the customer a service fee (flat base + add-on fees as applicable)
- Does NOT bid at auction, does NOT hold title, does NOT take payment for the vehicle itself

**Florida partner dealer (FL licensed IMD + SMVD)**
- Holds the active dealer account at IAA (and potentially Copart)
- Executes the auction bid and purchase under their license
- Briefly holds vehicle title during processing (hours to a few days)
- Reassigns/transfers title to the customer directly
- Does NOT retail vehicles under their license; does NOT act as the seller to the customer
- Charges Car Stable: **$100/year access fee + $200/auction win + IAA fees at cost**

**Customer**
- Commissions Car Stable to find and coordinate the purchase of a specific vehicle
- Pays the FL partner (or directly to auction) for the vehicle (auction price + auction fees)
- Pays Car Stable separately for the service fee
- Receives title in their name (or their lender's name if financing)
- Retitles in their home state

### Deal flow end to end

```
1. Customer submits intake form on carstable.com (vehicle specs, budget, condition tolerance)
2. Car Stable searches live auction inventory, delivers shortlist within 3-7 business days
3. Customer approves a specific vehicle and confirms authorization to bid
4. Customer funds the estimated auction buy price to [escrow / FL partner / Car Stable per attorney-advised structure]
5. Car Stable instructs FL partner on which vehicle to bid; FL partner executes the bid under their dealer account
6. FL partner wins the auction
   — FL partner charges Car Stable: $200 win fee + actual IAA fees (pass-through)
   — Car Stable passes these through to the customer at exact cost
7. Title is in FL partner's name briefly (processing period — hours to a few days)
8. FL partner reassigns/transfers title to the customer's name (or lender's name)
   — For branded titles: rebuilt-title inspection must be completed before customer retitles in their home state
9. Vehicle transported to rebuilder (if rebuild add-on) or directly to customer
10. Customer pays Car Stable's service fee (base + any add-ons)
11. Car Stable provides 30 days of post-delivery title/DMV support
```

### FL partner fee structure (confirmed)

| Fee | Amount | What it covers |
|-----|--------|----------------|
| Annual access fee | **$100/year** | Active dealer account access for Car Stable to use FL partner's IAA account |
| Per-win documentation fee | **$200/vehicle** | FL partner's admin and documentation for each auction win |
| IAA fees | **Pass-through at exact cost** | Buyer's fee, gate fee, storage (if any) — exactly what IAA charges, no markup |

Car Stable passes all FL partner costs through to the customer at exact cost — no markup on these either. Our revenue is solely the service fee.

**Annual FL partner cost at 24 deals/year:** $100 + (24 × $200) = **$4,900** + IAA pass-through. Modest for the dealer-account access it provides.

### Year 0 compliance rules

1. **Car Stable LLC never takes vehicle title.** Title stays at auction → FL partner → customer. The LLC's name never appears on a vehicle title or bill of sale as buyer or seller.
2. **Car Stable charges for services, not vehicles.** Service fee invoices describe "vehicle sourcing and concierge coordination services" — not the vehicle purchase.
3. **Customer-to-vehicle payment flow is separate from customer-to-Car-Stable fee payment.** The customer's vehicle money flows to the FL partner or directly to the auction; the service fee flows to Car Stable LLC separately.
4. **FL partner's role is execution, not retail.** The FL partner is acting as a buying agent / licensed dealer executing a purchase on commission — not as the retail seller to the customer.
5. **No "dealer" claims in Year 0 marketing.** Car Stable is a "concierge car buying service" or "auto buying platform" — not a dealership. No GDN number listed. No dealer-specific disclosures (Buyer's Guide, etc.).
6. **Attorney sign-off before launch.** Written legal opinion that the Year 0 structure does not require a Texas dealer or broker license. This is non-negotiable.

### Year 0 open questions (resolve before launch)

1. **Attorney review** — Texas dealer-compliance attorney written opinion: does this model require any Texas license?
2. **Customer payment flow** — does vehicle money go: (a) customer → FL partner directly, (b) customer → escrow → FL partner, or (c) customer → Car Stable (as agent) → FL partner? Each has different licensing and tax implications.
3. **Written agreement with FL partner** — auction-access scope; $100/yr + $200/win + IAA pass-through; title-transfer mechanics; termination; Car Stable's service-company role explicitly defined; liability allocation.
4. **FL partner's comfort with this structure** — they're acting as a licensed buying agent, not a retail seller. Does their license and business model support this? Does their insurance cover it?
5. **Financing referral licensing** — some states require a license to charge a fee for referring customers to lenders. Texas requirement: TBD.
6. **Copart access** — same partner, separate arrangement, or different partner?
7. **Volume pacing** — attorney may recommend staying below a monthly threshold while the structure is new; get their guidance.

### Triggers to exit Year 0

- Attorney confirms or refines the structure, and we're ready to scale
- Volume hits ~10+ deals/month consistently and it makes sense to have our own dealer account
- FL partner relationship becomes a limiting factor (volume, geography, or terms)
- We want to add Manheim or ADESA access (Manheim requires dealer account in the buying dealer's state)
- Year 1 capital ($10-25K licensing cost) is on hand

## Year 1+ — Car Stable's Own Texas GDN

### Why we need a dealer license eventually

Once Car Stable buys at auction and briefly holds title — even for hours — before reassigning to the customer, we are technically **buying and reselling a vehicle**. That is the statutory definition of a dealer in Texas (and virtually every other state). We need a Texas Independent Motor Vehicle GDN.

### The no-inventory GDN

Car Stable's model does not require holding inventory. Vehicles flow: auction → Car Stable's dealer account → customer. The title hold is brief. This is a legal and valid way to operate under a GDN — many dealer licensees do not hold inventory. However, Texas's **physical location requirements still apply** even if you're not parking cars on a lot.

### Physical location reality

TxDMV requires a compliant commercial location as the "established place of business." Key requirements:

| Requirement | Compliant options for Car Stable's model |
|-------------|------------------------------------------|
| Commercially zoned address | Commercial office suite, shared workspace in a commercial building, sublet space from an existing dealer |
| ≥100 sq ft interior | Easily met by any commercial suite |
| Not a home, hotel, or virtual office | Home office does not qualify |
| Permanent exterior sign (≥6-inch letters) | Required even on a shared building |
| Display area for ≥5 vehicles | **The tricky one** — Texas requires space to display 5 vehicles; for a no-inventory model, this space can be an adjacent lot, leased overflow area, or shared dealer lot, as long as it's part of the licensed location |
| Certificate of Occupancy | Required |

**Practical option:** Sublease space from an existing DFW dealer (many are open to this) — you get the compliant address and display area without a standalone lot lease. Cost: $500-$1,500/month typically, vs. $2,000-$5,000/month for a standalone compliant lot.

## Option C — Full Car Stable Texas Licensing Checklist (Year 1+)

Car Stable needs **one or two** Texas licenses depending on the vehicle mix:

1. **Independent Motor Vehicle GDN** — required to buy and resell any vehicle, including clean-title
2. **Salvage Vehicle Dealer License** — required if sourcing/rebuilding/reselling more than 5 salvage/nonrepairable vehicles per year (very likely once the rebuild add-on is active)

### Step 1 — Entity & tax foundation

- [ ] **Texas LLC formation** — $300 with Secretary of State (likely already done)
- [ ] **Operating agreement** — defines owner + TX business partner roles, capital, profit, exit
- [ ] **Federal EIN** — free from IRS online
- [ ] **Texas Franchise Tax account** — free registration
- [ ] **Texas Sales and Use Tax Permit** — free; required before GDN application (note: in a concierge model where we're briefly holding title and reselling, we are technically making a sale and may owe sales tax — attorney and CPA to advise)
- [ ] **Assumed Name Certificate (DBA)** if entity name differs from "Car Stable" — $25-$50

### Step 2 — Physical location

- [ ] **Identify compliant commercial address in DFW** (sublet from existing dealer preferred)
- [ ] **Lease or sublease agreement** naming Car Stable at the address
- [ ] **Certificate of Occupancy**
- [ ] **Display area** (may be part of the host dealer's lot under sublease)
- [ ] **Permanent exterior sign** — letters ≥6 inches tall, weather-resistant
- [ ] **Office setup:** desk, two chairs, internet, phone listed in business name
- [ ] **Locked storage** for dealer plates

### Step 3 — Pre-licensing education

- [ ] **TxDMV-approved pre-licensing course** — ~6 hours online; $100-$200

### Step 4 — Surety bond

- [ ] **$50,000 Motor Vehicle Dealer Surety Bond** (2-year term)
  - Premium: 1-3%/year → ~$500-$1,500/year
  - Must list legal business name + licensed address

### Step 5 — Independent Motor Vehicle GDN application

- [ ] Submit via **TxDMV eLICENSING portal**
- [ ] Upload: entity docs, tax permits, lease/deed, Certificate of Occupancy, location photos, education certificate, surety bond, criminal background check, ID
- [ ] Pay license fee — ~$700 for 2-year term
- [ ] Pass unannounced TxDMV site inspection
- [ ] Receive GDN number

### Step 6 — Salvage Vehicle Dealer License (if applicable)

- [ ] Separate application via eLICENSING
- [ ] **$190 initial / $170 renewal** (2-year term)
- [ ] Same entity/tax/location prerequisites as GDN

### Step 7 — Dealer accounts & operational tools

- [ ] **IAA dealer account** — apply directly once GDN is in hand; lower per-deal cost than going through the FL partner
- [ ] **Copart dealer account** — separate application
- [ ] **Manheim/ADESA account** (Year 1+ expansion) — requires dealer license in buying state
- [ ] **Metal dealer plates** — $90 each / 2-year term
- [ ] **eTAG / buyer temp tag account** via TxDMV (needed even for pass-through model — any vehicle we hold, even briefly, needs to be insured and tagged)
- [ ] **webDEALER account** for title processing
- [ ] **Garage liability insurance** — ~$1,500-$5,000/year
- [ ] **Dealer inventory insurance** — covers the brief period we hold title on each vehicle; can often be structured as a "floorplan lite" or "in-transit" policy rather than traditional floorplan (since we never have more than 1-3 vehicles in title at a time)
- [ ] **DMS / deal-tracking software** — lightweight at launch (Frazer, vAuto, or even a well-structured spreadsheet + DocuSign); full DMS as volume grows

**Revenue needed to break even:** 2-3 deals/month at a $1,000 base fee covers overhead. Profitable at 5+ deals/month.
